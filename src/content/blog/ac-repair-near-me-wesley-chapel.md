<h2>The Difference Between AC Repair and AC Replacement, and Why It Matters</h2>

<p>One of the most important decisions you will make when your AC breaks down is whether to repair or replace. Not every contractor will walk you through this honestly. Some companies make more money on new installations and will steer you toward replacement even when a repair would solve the problem at a fraction of the cost.</p>

<p>A reliable contractor will explain the options clearly. If your system is relatively new and the repair cost is manageable, they will tell you that. If your system is older and the repair cost is approaching the threshold where replacement makes more long-term financial sense, they will show you that math too, without pressuring you in either direction.</p>

<p>The rule of thumb most homeowners find useful: multiply the age of your system by the cost of the repair. If that number exceeds $5,000, replacement often becomes the smarter choice. But every situation is different, and your specific circumstances matter more than any general rule.</p>
